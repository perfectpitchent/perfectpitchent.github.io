Puerto Rican artist and healer, María Jose Montijo, who performs under her musical moniker Esotérica Tropical, is set to release her self-titled debut album, Esotérica Tropical, on September 6, 2024. This album transforms the struggles of living amidst ongoing crises into a vibrant celebration of resilience and freedom.

Written after her move to Oakland, California, the album reflects the deep longing of Puerto Ricans worldwide to reconnect with their homeland. Its ten tracks are heartfelt love letters to Puerto Rico, drawing on ancestral roots and the power of remembrance.

“Esotérica Tropical is about embracing the natural world, listening to the elements, and channeling ancestral wisdom to transform pain into joy,” Esoterica explains. “It’s music that is medicinal, political, and deeply personal.”

The album was recorded at Zoo Labs Studios, a non-profit artist incubator in Oakland that supports BIPOC artists, and blends traditional bomba rhythms with modern production. Collaborators include Luis Maurette, Heidi Lewandowski, Adam Partridge, and indie music powerhouse Merrill Garbus of Tune-Yards.

“Esotérica Tropical is a tribute to the inner work I’ve done to thrive and help others heal,” Esotérica Tropical shares. “These songs are designed to nourish and empower, especially for QTBIPOC communities, offering a path toward authenticity, alignment, and sovereignty.”

Esotérica Tropical is a queer Boricua bruja who transcends musical boundaries with her commanding voice, intuitive folk harp, and a fusion of Afro-Puerto Rican Bomba rhythms and electronic innovation. Her work boldly challenges colonial mentality, advocating for sovereignty and surrealism. A chance encounter with a Celtic harp on a Puerto Rican beach in 2009 transformed her from an acupuncturist into a genre-defying musical magician. Drawing on 20 years as a healer, Esotérica’s ritualistic performances have captivated audiences at major venues and festivals worldwide, from the Oakland Museum to the Teatro Municipal de Cusco. In 2022, she featured on Argentinian producer Chancha Via Circuito’s album La Estrella.
